Outreach / Sales

Tone Pillars

  • Specific over vague — "a radiologist reading the echo" beats "domain expert." Name the person, name the task.
  • Honest about tradeoffs — we're not for everyone. Say so. It builds trust with the people we are for.
  • No superlatives — no "best-in-class," no "seamlessly," no exclamation marks. The work makes the case.
  • Founder voice — write as if Nawaf is typing a reply, not a marketing team drafting a brief.

We Are / We're Not

  • A small team that staffs expert annotators — not a platform you self-serve
  • The right call for domain-heavy tasks — not for high-volume, low-complexity work
  • Honest about timelines and fit — not a yes to every RFP
  • Quality over throughput — which is why we're more expensive than crowd platforms
  • Early stage — which means you get direct access to the founders, not a CSM

Write Like This

"Radiology reports labeled by radiologists. Drug interactions reviewed by pharmacologists. If the annotation requires a degree, we staff the team accordingly."

Not Like This

"We offer best-in-class AI data annotation solutions powered by a community of verified domain experts to help you unlock the full potential of your ML pipeline!"
